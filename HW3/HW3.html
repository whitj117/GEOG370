In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

I first downloaded a plug in for QGIS, which enabled me to put caps and graticules onto the natural earth vector. I was able to find different projections in the bottom right corner or QGIS.

WGS84 Projection

More accurate towards the equator, less so towards the poles.

Aitoff Projection

Almost spherical. Less distortion towards the equator and prime meridian.

Pseudo-Mercator Projection

It distorts in a similar manner to the Mercator projection, with the poles being enlarged.

Sphere Winkel Projection

Being closer to a spherical shape, the sizes are more accurate. Still some distortion closer to the poles but not as bad as the Mercator.

World Cylindrical Equal Area Projection

Sizes are more accurate closer to the poles than Mercator, but the shape is more distorted, flattening land masses closer to the poles.

World Equidistant Conic Projection

Vastly increases the size of the poles. Bird's eye view from the North Pole. Fairly accurate shapes toward the North Pole.

North Pole Azimuthal Equidistant Projection

Rather exaggerated sizes, decreasing in exaggeration further north. decent view of all continents, though Australia and Antarctica are quite enlarged.

World Goode Homolosine Land Projection

Greatly exaggerates/distorts Greenland and Antarctica, though everything else is rather accurate in terms of size and shape.

WGS 1984 ARC System Zone 17

The continents appear much closer together than with other projections. Similar to the Mercator projection, but sort of squished together.
